    But Irina had a secret passion that no one knew about. She loved gardening. She loved the smell of fresh flowers, the feel of soil in her hands, the sight of green leaves and colorful petals. She wanted to learn how to grow **Lemon Balm and China Aster**, two of her favorite plants. She had read many books and articles about them, but she wanted to experience them for herself.

    **Lemon Balm** (Melissa officinalis) is a herbaceous perennial plant that belongs to the mint family. It has a lemony scent and flavor, and is used for culinary and medicinal purposes. It can help with stress, insomnia, anxiety, and digestive problems. It can also attract bees and butterflies to the garden.

    **China Aster** (Callistephus chinensis) is an annual flower that belongs to the aster family. It has large, showy blooms in various colors, such as red, pink, purple, white, and blue. It can brighten up any garden or container with its cheerful flowers from summer to fall. It can also make long-lasting cut flower arrangements.

    She also had other hobbies that she enjoyed in her spare time. She liked to play chess, paint landscapes, and write poems. She found these activities relaxing and stimulating, as they challenged her mind and expressed her creativity. She had a collection of chess sets, paints, and notebooks that she treasured.

    So she decided to join a gardening club and an art club at her school, which was called **Magnolia Academy**. It was a prestigious school for gifted students, where Irina excelled in every subject. There she met Lily and Lisa, two girls who shared her interest in plants and arts. They were friendly and cheerful, and they welcomed Irina with open arms. They taught her how to sow seeds, water plants, prune branches, and harvest flowers. They also taught her how to mix colors, brush strokes, and rhyme words. They also shared stories, jokes, and secrets with her. Irina felt happy and relaxed with them. She felt like she had found true friends.

    Irina realized that there was more to life than being smart. She learned to appreciate the simple joys of nature, the warmth of friendship, and the beauty of diversity. She found a balance between her intellect and her emotions, between her achievements and her hobbies, between her solitude and her social life. She found herself.
